About & Help Information

This page has more detailed information about the network, and any help materials that might be needed.

About FUBUnet

Ridgewood FUBUnet is a community-managed listing service created by and for local residents. Unlike corporate platforms, we don't collect data, show ads, or prioritize listings based on payment. All posts are treated equally and moderated by community volunteers.

This location is managed by Tiny Arts Supply, who provides the hosting and technical support for the platform. Other neighborhoods may have their own FUBUnet instances managed by different local organizations.

How to Use FUBUnet

Browse listings by clicking on the category tabs at the top of the page. Click on any listing to view its details. To return to listings, simply click on a category tab again.

To post a listing, please visit Tiny Arts Supply during business hours with your content prepared. A staff member will help you create your post for a small fee ($2) which helps maintain the service.

Community Guidelines

• Be respectful and honest in all listings

• No discriminatory content or language

• No illegal services or items

• Housing posts must comply with fair housing laws

• Jobs must list compensation information

• Sale items must be accurately described

Ridgewood Mutual Aid is organizing our annual winter coat giveaway at Madison Park. We have collected over 200 coats in various sizes for adults and children that will be distributed to community members in need.

If you have coats to donate, you can bring them to the event or drop them off at Tiny Arts Supply during business hours before January 16th.

Location: Madison Park gazebo • Contact: winter@ridgewoodmutualaid.org

Community garden planning meeting

January 25, 2025

Join us for the first planning meeting of the year for the Ridgewood Community Garden. We'll be discussing plans for the upcoming growing season, assigning plots, and organizing volunteer schedules.

This meeting is open to both current garden members and those interested in joining. We'll cover the basics of community garden participation, membership fees, and responsibilities. New members are welcome as we have several plots opening up this year.

The meeting will be held indoors at the community center at 7pm. Light refreshments will be provided, and children are welcome.

Location: Ridgewood Community Center, Room 3B • Contact: grow@greenridgewood.org

Brooklyn Ave street cleanup

February 3, 2025

Volunteers needed for our monthly street cleanup, focusing on Brooklyn Avenue from Madison to Jefferson. We'll be picking up litter, sweeping sidewalks, and removing graffiti where possible.

Cleanup supplies including gloves, bags, and tools will be provided. Please wear weather-appropriate clothing and sturdy shoes. The cleanup will run from 10am to 1pm, but volunteers are welcome to participate for any amount of time within that window.

This is a great opportunity to meet neighbors and make a visible improvement to our community. Students can earn community service hours.

Meeting point: Corner of Brooklyn Ave & Madison St • Contact: cleanup@cleanridgewood.org
